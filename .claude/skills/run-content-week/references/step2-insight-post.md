---

## 2B: Extract One Surprising Data Point

From the case study, find:
- One specific number or finding that challenges a common assumption
- The business implication for an SME owner

This is NOT a summary of the case study. It is ONE finding that stands alone.

Good: "We analysed 12 months of sales data. Tuesday afternoon outsells Saturday morning by 23%."
Weak: "Data analysis can help you understand your business better."

---

## 2C: Write the Post

Write a LinkedIn post following this structure (150–250 words):

1. **Hook (lines 1–2):** The counterintuitive finding. Must work before "see more" cutoff.
2. **Context (1 paragraph):** Why this matters. What assumption does the reader probably hold?
3. **The data point (1–2 paragraphs):** The specific finding with the number.
4. **Implication (1 sentence):** What should the reader check in their own business?
5. **CTA (1 line):** Low-pressure. No blog link. Example: "Check your own numbers. The answer might surprise you."
6. **Hashtags:** 3–5 relevant hashtags.

Apply brand voice rules:
- Short sentences, everyday words, specific numbers
- No jargon: algorithm, cohort, regression, ML, AI, ETL
- No filler: leverage, synergies, holistic, end-to-end
- Active voice, "you/your" addressing the reader

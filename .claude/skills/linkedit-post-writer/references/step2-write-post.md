# Step 2 — Write the LinkedIn Post

## What This Step Does

Write the LinkedIn post using the hook angle, key data point, and blog URL confirmed in Step 1. The post earns the click — it does not give away the full answer.

---

## 2A — The Most Important Rule: The Hook Line

LinkedIn cuts off post text after 2–3 lines with a "see more" button. Everything after that is hidden until the reader actively clicks.

The first 1–2 lines must work completely on their own. If those lines are not interesting, the rest of the post will never be read.

**The hook must be one of these types:**

| Type | Example |
|---|---|
| Counterintuitive statement | "Your highest-revenue reseller may be your most expensive liability." |
| Provocative question | "What if the products your team is pushing hardest are the ones quietly shrinking your margin?" |
| Specific surprising fact | "We analysed 60,000 orders. The reseller with the biggest discounts returned negative $1,250 in gross margin." |
| Challenge a common belief | "Most manufacturers track resellers by revenue. That is the wrong number." |

Do not open with:
- A greeting ("Hi LinkedIn friends")
- A generic statement ("Data is important for businesses today")
- A question that is too vague ("Have you ever wondered about your data?")

---

## 2B — Full Post Structure

Write the post in this exact sequence. Keep it to 150–250 words total.

**Part 1 — Hook (lines 1–2)**
The counterintuitive statement or surprising fact. This is what shows before "see more". Make it earn the click.

**Part 2 — Context (1 short paragraph)**
One or two sentences that set up why this matters. What is the common mistake or assumption the reader is probably making right now?

**Part 3 — The key finding (1–2 short paragraphs)**
The specific data point from the blog. One concrete number or outcome. Do not explain the full analysis — just enough to make the reader feel they are missing something.

**Part 4 — The implication (1 sentence)**
What does this mean for the reader's own business? Keep it direct. No jargon.

**Part 5 — CTA**
One line directing to the blog. Keep it low-pressure.

English: `Read the full breakdown here: [blog URL]`
Mandarin: `完整分析在这里：[blog URL]`

**Part 6 — Hashtags**
3–5 hashtags on the final line. No more.

English posts: `#analytics #SME #Malaysia #businessgrowth #sales` (adjust to topic)
Mandarin posts: `#数据分析 #中小企业 #马来西亚 #生意成长` (adjust to topic)

---

## 2C — Mandarin Post Rules

When the blog is in Mandarin, write the LinkedIn post in Mandarin:

- Use 马来西亚华语 — conversational, grounded, not formal or academic
- Keep sentences short — Mandarin reads better in short bursts on social media
- Use 您 when addressing the reader
- Numbers stay as numerals (60%, not 百分之六十)
- The hook must still be counterintuitive or surprising — the same rule applies regardless of language
- Do not translate the English structure word for word — write natively

---

## 2D — What to Leave Out

The LinkedIn post must NOT include:

- The full analysis or methodology
- All the data points from the blog (one is enough)
- The conclusion or the answer to the problem
- Any mention of pricing or services
- The word "analytics" used as filler

The reader should finish the post thinking: "That's interesting — I want to know how they figured that out." That curiosity is what drives the click.
